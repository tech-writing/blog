What is Software, Platform, and Infrastructure as a Service

Understand and compare three most popular cloud computing service models - SaaS, PaaS, IaaS.


Traditionally companies and individuals consume hardware, software, and other IT assets by buying and installing them. In the “as a service” model cloud vendors own all assets and equipment and customers rent it with an on-demand approach.

Software as a Service (SaaS)

Idea of SaaS is to provide you software through your web browser as a user interface instead of downloading and installing the same software locally on your machine. For example, a well known text processor Microsoft Office can be installed locally on your computer. Alternatively, you can use Microsoft Office online with your browser as an interface. In this case a stable Internet connection is required.

At this model you pay monthly subscription instead of one time payment when you buy and download the software distributive. At the same time with SaaS you utilize less resources on your computer, as all data and calculations are related to the server side of the service provider. Also you don’t need to think about installing and configuring software on your computer.


Platform as a Service (PaaS) and Infrastructure as a Service (IaaS)

PaaS and IaaS are mostly oriented on software engineers and require technical skills to utilize its functionality. PaaS aims to create a professional cloud environment for software engineers to effectively build, test, run, host, scale their applications. It includes all variety of services like operating systems, runtime, virtualization, middleware, servers, storage, and networking.

IaaS is focused on providing the solutions to serve IT backend infrastructure like physical and virtual servers, storage and networking. SaaS and IaaS are being offered by technology giants like Microsoft, Google, Amazon, IBM, etc.
