Types of Business Software Applications

Discover most known types of software applications that businesses use (CRM, ERP, CMS, DBMS, CAD) and what solutions do they provide.


CRM

CRM stands for customer relationship management. This software is dedicated for any business to aid account, manage, analyze and automate relationships both with current and potential customers. Main purpose of the CRM is to force sales, find out insights, and better satisfy customers needs.

There are lots of options on the market like Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics CRM, SAP CRM, Zoho, Twilio and many others that provide customizable functionality and prices for their clients. Typically these services include contacts management, tracking sales leads, workflow automations, integrations with third-party software, analytics, and many vendors also include tools for marketing campaigns.


ERP

An enterprise resource planning (ERP) aims to provide mid to large companies tools to effectively deal with their resources and business processes. ERP may provide modules for manufacturing, service, logistics and supply chain, procurement, asset management, finance, human resources, etc.

ERP software targets to break down the barriers between the front office and back office, and offer the ability to adapt solutions to new business priorities. Some examples can include SAP ERP, Oracle ERP, Microsoft Dynamics ERP, and others.


CMS

CMS is an abbreviation for content management system. This software is used to manage the creation and modification of digital content, usually related to web sites and platforms. CMS solutions typically include automated web templates, access control, scalable options, easily editable content and workflow management. Finally it aims for businesses to automate and scale their web solutions and content, increase traffic, and be focused on personalization customer experience instead of technical issues. Some popular CMS platforms are WordPress, Drupal, Shopify, Joomla, etc.


DBMS

A database management system (DBSM) is simply a data-keeping software. It targets to provide tools for storing and retrieving data that businesses need to operate. Usually it provides a graphical user interface and ability to query data with structured query language (SQL).

These solutions are more technical and require more special skills and knowledge to work with. Typically DBMS are used by analytics to retrieve actual and relevant data to help businesses make right decisions. DBMS can be provided on-premises or as cloud databases, relational and non-relational. Examples can include Oracle, Google, Microsoft, Azure, IBM, Amazon cloud services for database management as well as on-premises solutions.


CAD

A computer-aid design or CAD is an engineering software to develop and visualize technical solutions in manufacturing, building, automotive, electrical, etc. industries. It provides 2D and 3D perspectives for designers, engineers and architects. Most known solutions are Autodesk AutoCAD, Revit, Civil 3D, etc.
